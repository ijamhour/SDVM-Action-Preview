# Handoff Ambiguity — PRE

## Situation

A synthetic multi-stage workflow (intake through finalize) runs on a recurring schedule. On paper, work progresses stage by stage. In practice, downstream actors often lack the context upstream actors assumed was obvious.

Handoff points are implicit: no standard recap, no explicit owner for open decisions, and no shared record of what was agreed before transfer.

## Observable evidence pattern

- Work moves forward, then returns for clarification after a handoff.
- Review or downstream stages repeat questions that earlier stages should have settled.
- Evidence shows transitions between stages without a consistent “handoff complete” signal.
- The declared workflow map may not list every transition teams actually use — making it hard to tell whether ambiguity is behavioral or documentary.

## Public-safe reading

This PRE window suggests **workflow friction** at handoff boundaries. Context loss is visible as repeated re-orientation after transfers, not as a single failed run.

Readers should ask whether handoffs are **contracted** (who owns what, what was decided, what remains open) or merely **logged** (a stage change occurred).
