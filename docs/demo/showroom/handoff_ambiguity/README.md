# Handoff Ambiguity

> This is a synthetic/sanitized public-safe showroom example. It is not generated by the public Action Preview and does not expose the private SDVM engine, scoring internals, calibration, thresholds, weights, statistical methodology, or playbook logic.

## What this scenario illustrates

A recurring workflow shows unclear transfer of responsibility between roles or stages. Work appears to move forward, but later review reveals that important context or decision ownership was not carried across the handoff.

In this synthetic example, transitions between stages look valid on the surface, yet reviewers and downstream actors repeatedly re-ask questions that should have been settled earlier. The workflow map and the lived process may disagree about which handoffs are expected, allowed, or complete.

## PRE

Before adjustment, handoffs lack explicit contracts. Actors pass work along without a shared recap of decisions, constraints, or open questions. Evidence patterns suggest repeated clarification after transfers — a sign that context did not travel with the work.

## Public-safe interpretation

Handoff ambiguity is a **coordination pathology**, not a single failed task. Public-safe reading focuses on whether evidence shows context traveling across stage boundaries — not on ranking severity or prescribing a specific fix.

A useful pilot question: *Do handoffs include enough shared context that the receiving actor can continue without reopening settled decisions?*

## How to discuss this with a pilot sponsor

1. **Name the pattern** — handoff ambiguity, not “bad handoffs” in general.
2. **Point to evidence shape** — recurring clarification after stage transitions in authorized traces.
3. **Separate map from behavior** — distinguish incomplete workflow documentation from genuine process drift.
4. **Propose a controlled pilot** — compare PRE and POST windows after agreed capture and handoff-contract changes.
5. **Set expectations** — full SDVM analysis remains in authorized controlled pilot work only.
