Candidate findings for pilot discussion

Candidate finding 1

What it may mean

Receiving stage may lack observable handoff context — or context may exist but not be captured in Tier 2 evidence.

What would strengthen attribution

Handoff summaries, owner fields, and reason codes at each transfer point.

Candidate finding 2
Ownership signals are thin at transfer points
What it may mean

Responsibility boundaries may be unclear — or may be clear in practice but absent from logs.

What would strengthen attribution

Explicit owner/receiver metadata and closure criteria per stage.

Candidate finding 3
Declared map and observed movement may diverge
What it may mean

Friction may reflect map mismatch rather than handoff failure.

What would strengthen attribution

Comparable traces with stage labels and transition timestamps.

Pilot discussion plan

Evidence planning prompts — not intervention prescription.

  1. Identify which transitions concentrate friction signals.
  2. Check whether reason codes or handoff summaries are available.
  3. Separate normal review behavior from repeated clarification.
  4. Add or request minimal handoff evidence fields for a controlled comparison.
  5. Treat any pathology label as provisional until comparable traces confirm it.
Attribution boundary
Tier 2-style evidence can locate friction and formulate candidate explanations. It cannot, by itself, distinguish ambiguous responsibility from insufficient context, normal review behavior, incomplete workflow mapping, or instrumentation gaps.
Interpretation limit
This static illustration supports operational discussion from qualitative evidence patterns. Stronger attribution requires enriched decision, handoff, and trace evidence in authorized controlled pilot work — not available in the public preview repository.

  • DELTA — Qualitative comparison between PRE and POST.
