PRE / POST / DELTA

PRE

PRE enriched traces show that handoff summaries and decision-status fields were missing or incomplete at transfer points. Downstream clarification was associated with missing context rather than only with normal review movement.

POST

POST enriched traces show clearer handoff summaries, explicit owner/receiver metadata, and fewer clarification reason codes after targeted handoff repairs were applied.

DELTA

The DELTA readout supports an observed reduction in handoff friction and stronger mechanism-level attribution. The candidate mechanism — incomplete context transfer at handoff — is corroborated within this scenario, while broader generalization still requires additional comparable traces.

Mechanism-level support

Support 1
Context transfer became observable
What changed

Handoff summaries and evidence references appear consistently in POST enriched traces.

Why it supports the candidate mechanism

Missing context at transfer points can be linked to specific absent fields rather than inferred from movement alone.

What still remains bounded

Other workflows or stages may still lack comparable capture; generalization requires broader traces.

Support 2
Responsibility boundary became explicit
What changed

Owner/receiver metadata is recorded at Draft → Review handoff in POST.

Why it supports the candidate mechanism

Ambiguous ownership is less plausible when explicit transfer roles are present in enriched evidence.

What still remains bounded

Behavioral compliance with declared roles is not fully observable from trace shape alone.

Support 3
Review movement became more decision-oriented
What changed

Reason codes shift from clarification-heavy to decision-oriented across PRE and POST.

Why it supports the candidate mechanism

Enriched evidence supports reading reduced friction as linked to repaired handoff context, not only process change.

What still remains bounded

Legitimate review depth and external factors may still contribute; mechanism is corroborated, not exclusive.

Evidence validation plan

Questions to test whether the enriched mechanism-level readout holds across broader comparable traces.

  1. Confirm enriched fields are consistently captured across comparable runs.
  2. Compare PRE/POST clarification reason codes and handoff summaries.
  3. Check whether decision context is preserved across the highlighted handoff boundary.
  4. Test whether the observed improvement persists beyond the curated scenario.
  5. Promote the mechanism from scenario-corroborated to stronger deployment-level attribution only after broader comparable evidence supports it.
Attribution boundary
Tier 3-style enriched evidence can strengthen mechanism-level attribution by connecting observed friction to specific missing or repaired handoff evidence. It still does not prove a sole root cause. Broader attribution requires additional comparable traces and deployment-specific review.

Public-safe glossary
  • Tier 2-style readout — Qualitative friction signals and candidate interpretations without mechanism-level corroboration.
  • Tier 3-style enriched readout — Stronger attribution when handoff, decision, and trace fields are available.
  • Handoff summary — Short recap of context transferred between stages.
  • Owner / receiver — Explicit roles at a handoff boundary.
  • Decision status — Whether prior decisions are carried into the receiving stage.
  • Reason code — Qualitative label for why review or clarification occurred.
  • Mechanism-level attribution — Connecting friction to a specific evidence-backed mechanism — stronger, not absolute.
  • Corroborated candidate mechanism — A candidate explanation supported by enriched PRE/POST evidence within a scenario.
