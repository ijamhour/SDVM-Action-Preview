PRE / POST / DELTA

PRE

PRE signals are compatible with late-stage correction patterns, but Tier 2 evidence alone cannot tell whether checkpoints were absent, bypassed, or simply unlogged.

POST

POST traces show earlier or more explicit checkpointing after repair actions suggested from the PRE readout were applied. The improvement is observed within this PRE/POST scenario; what remains bounded is the causal attribution and whether the same pattern generalizes across broader comparable traces.

DELTA

The DELTA readout compares the PRE late-checkpoint pattern with the POST trace round after repairs. It supports an observed improvement in checkpoint timing within this scenario. This strengthens the candidate attribution while keeping the specific pathology label provisional under Tier 2 evidence.

Candidate findings for evidence follow-up

Candidate finding 1
Correction activity clusters near finalize
What it may mean

Checks may occur after commitment — or late validation may be intentional but unlogged.

What would strengthen attribution

Checkpoint timestamps and gate pass/fail markers before finalize.

Candidate finding 2
Multiple correction causes appear together
What it may mean

Rework may be hard to localize — or stage labels may be incomplete in Tier 2 evidence.

What would strengthen attribution

Stage-localized correction fields and reason codes across comparable runs.

Candidate finding 3
Checkpoint boundaries are thin in trace
What it may mean

Pass/fail criteria may not be recorded — or may exist outside observable logs.

What would strengthen attribution

Explicit validation signals at each stage transition.

Evidence follow-up plan

Questions to strengthen or refute the candidate attribution in a controlled comparison.

  1. Identify where correction work accumulates late in the workflow.
  2. Check whether checkpoint timestamps, checkpoint summaries, and reason codes are available.
  3. Separate legitimate late validation from avoidable late-stage rework.
  4. Add or request minimal checkpoint evidence fields for a controlled PRE/POST comparison.
  5. Treat the late-checkpoint label as provisional until comparable traces support it.
