# Late Checkpoint — PRE

## Situation

A synthetic workflow declares a canonical path through intake, retrieval, draft, review, and finalize. In practice, some runs **skip ahead** — moving from early stages directly toward finalize without passing through review or other agreed coordination checkpoints.

Late discovery of issues forces backward movement: work returns from finalize or late stages to earlier stages for correction. The workflow pays rework cost that earlier gates could have prevented.

## Observable evidence pattern

- Transitions that jump past expected intermediate stages appear across multiple runs.
- Corrections and clarification cluster near finalize rather than at planned gates.
- Backward movement from late stages to earlier stages shows up as repeated recovery work.
- It becomes hard to tell a one-off mistake from a systemic fragile transition habit.

## Public-safe reading

The PRE window illustrates **late checkpoint** friction: validation happens after commitment, not before. Readers should ask where the workflow allows progress without an explicit pass-through of agreed gates.

## What this does not claim

- Not a scored assessment of skip frequency or severity.
- No causal claim about why skips occur (policy, tooling, or habit).
- No numeric tables or diagnostic rankings.
- Not a full SDVM report or Action Preview output.
