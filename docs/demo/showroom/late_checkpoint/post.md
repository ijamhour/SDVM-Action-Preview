# Late Checkpoint — POST

## Situation after workflow adjustment

The team enforces pass-through at agreed checkpoints — especially before finalize. Stage transitions that bypass review or other gates are blocked or flagged in process, and traces increasingly show the canonical path unless a documented exception applies.

Early gates catch issues when they are cheaper to fix.

## Observable evidence pattern

- Fewer skip-ahead transitions toward finalize without intermediate validation.
- Review (or equivalent gates) appears consistently before late-stage commitment.
- Backward recovery from finalize decreases in trace narratives.
- Documented exceptions remain possible but are no longer the default path.

## Public-safe reading

POST traces show earlier or more explicit checkpointing after repair actions suggested from the PRE readout were applied. The improvement is **observed within this PRE/POST scenario** — a change in when coordination happens, not a quantified efficiency claim. What remains bounded is the causal attribution and whether the same pattern generalizes across broader comparable traces.

## What this does not claim

- Not proof that all late rework is eliminated.
- No intervention prescription from private engine logic.
- No statistical confidence or scored bands.
- Not generated by the public Action Preview.
