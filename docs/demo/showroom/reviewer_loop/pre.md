# Reviewer Loop — PRE

## Situation

A synthetic workflow routes all substantive work through draft and review stages before finalize. Reviewers are thorough; authors iterate. Over many runs, the pattern stabilizes into a **review loop**: work returns from review to draft repeatedly on comparable issues.

Upstream stages do not consistently preserve decisions, constraints, or rationale that reviewers need. Each review cycle partially re-derives context that should have traveled with the handoff.

## Observable evidence pattern

- Repeated transitions between draft and review across comparable runs.
- Rework clusters on the same stage boundary rather than spreading evenly.
- Review effort rises even when final outputs eventually pass.
- Finalize-stage recovery work appears when issues surface late.

## Public-safe reading

The PRE window shows **workflow friction** in the form of a reviewer loop — a coordination pathology where review amplifies upstream context loss. This is an evidence pattern discussion, not a performance review of individuals.

## What this does not claim

- Not a scored diagnostic or ranked severity assessment.
- No causal blame on reviewers, authors, or tools.
- No numeric rework metrics or benchmarks.
- Not output from the public Action Preview or a full SDVM report.
