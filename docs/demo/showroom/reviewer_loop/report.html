Candidate findings for pilot discussion

Candidate finding 1
Draft ↔ Review movement is repetitive
What it may mean

Upstream packages may lack observable context for focused review — or review depth may be intentional.

What would strengthen attribution

Review-readiness criteria and minimum upstream package fields.

Candidate finding 2
Clarification churn recurs at the same boundary
What it may mean

Comparable issues may reappear — or reason codes may be missing, making normal iteration look like friction.

What would strengthen attribution

Reason codes and return-to-draft markers across comparable runs.

Candidate finding 3
Decision carryover is thin in trace
What it may mean

Prior rationale may not be captured — or may exist outside Tier 2 observability.

What would strengthen attribution

Decision status fields and explicit rationale references before review.

Pilot discussion plan

Evidence planning prompts — not intervention prescription.

  1. Identify which transitions concentrate friction signals.
  2. Check whether reason codes or handoff summaries are available.
  3. Separate normal review behavior from repeated clarification.
  4. Add or request minimal handoff evidence fields for a controlled comparison.
  5. Treat any pathology label as provisional until comparable traces confirm it.
Attribution boundary
Tier 2-style evidence can locate friction and formulate candidate explanations. It cannot, by itself, distinguish ambiguous responsibility from insufficient context, normal review behavior, incomplete workflow mapping, or instrumentation gaps.
