# Reviewer Loop — POST

## Situation after workflow adjustment

The team strengthens upstream capture before review: explicit decision summaries, constraint lists, and handoff recaps at the draft-to-review boundary. Reviewers receive enough context to approve or reject on substance rather than reconstructing prior work.

Authors and reviewers share a minimal contract for what “ready for review” means.

## Observable evidence pattern

- Fewer round-trips between draft and review on comparable work items.
- Review cycles shorter in narrative trace — less back-and-forth clarification.
- Rework still occurs where appropriate, but not as a default loop on every run.
- Some residual loops may remain on edge cases or immature capture habits.

## Public-safe reading

The POST window suggests the **review loop loosened** when context travels earlier. Improvement is qualitative: the evidence pattern shows less oscillation, not a quantified efficiency gain.

## What this does not claim

- Not a guarantee that review is eliminated or always single-pass.
- No intervention prescription from private engine logic.
- No statistical or scored comparison to PRE.
