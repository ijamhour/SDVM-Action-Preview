PRE
Remaining friction

Review repeatedly sends work back for clarification. The final output may still be acceptable, but review effort grows because earlier stages fail to preserve enough context.

POST
Improved clarity

Earlier stages preserve more context and open questions are surfaced sooner. Review loops become more focused on substance rather than reconstructing missing information.

DELTA
Public-safe reading

Review becomes less repetitive and more decision-oriented. Remaining issue: some context gaps may still require better upstream capture habits.

What changed

Draft-to-review handoffs strengthened with explicit decision summaries and a shared definition of “ready for review.” Trace narratives show less oscillation between draft and review on comparable work items.

What remains unresolved

  • Edge cases and new work types may still trigger loops until contracts mature.
  • PRE and POST windows must stay comparable for meaningful discussion.
  • Instrumentation must capture stage intent consistently, not only stage names.
